This policy outlines the requirements for customer due diligence (CDD) procedures for retail banking staff when onboarding new customers or reviewing existing relationships. New customers must provide valid government-issued ID and address verification using utility bills or bank statements not older than three months. Customers are classified into low, medium, or high risk categories, with high-risk customers requiring enhanced due diligence procedures, including source of funds verification and senior management approval for account opening. All CDD records must be maintained securely for a minimum of seven years and made available to regulatory authorities upon request.
