• This policy establishes the requirements for customer due diligence procedures to be followed by all retail banking staff when onboarding new customers or reviewing existing customer relationships.
• The policy applies to all retail banking operations, including savings accounts, current accounts, fixed deposits, personal loans, and credit card applications, covering both individual and corporate customers.
• All new customers must provide valid government-issued photo identification, such as a passport, national identity card, or driving license.
• Address verification must be completed using utility bills or bank statements not older than three months for all new customers.
• Corporate customers must additionally provide a certificate of incorporation, memorandum of association, and board resolution.
• Customers are classified into three risk categories: low risk, medium risk, and high risk based on factors such as income source and credit history.
• High-risk customers require enhanced due diligence procedures, including source of funds verification, senior management approval for account opening, and more frequent transaction monitoring and reviews.
• All customer due diligence records must be maintained for a minimum period of seven years from the date of account closure.
• All staff involved in customer onboarding must complete mandatory AML training annually to ensure compliance with this policy.
• Branch managers are responsible for ensuring compliance with this policy within their respective branches.
