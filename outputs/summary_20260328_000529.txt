Here is a structured summary of the document:

**1. PURPOSE**
This document establishes the requirements for customer due diligence (CDD) procedures to be followed by retail banking staff when onboarding new customers or reviewing existing relationships.

**2. KEY POINTS**

* New customers must provide valid government-issued photo identification and address verification using utility bills or bank statements not older than three months.
* Corporate customers must provide additional documents, including certificate of incorporation, memorandum of association, and board resolution.
* Customers are classified into three risk categories: low, medium, and high risk.
* High-risk customers require enhanced due diligence procedures, including source of funds verification and senior management approval for account opening.

**3. WHO IT AFFECTS**
All retail banking staff involved in customer onboarding, as well as branch managers who are responsible for ensuring compliance with this policy within their respective branches.

**4. ACTION ITEMS**

* Retail banking staff must complete mandatory AML training annually.
* Branch managers must ensure compliance with this policy within their respective branches.
* All customer due diligence records must be maintained for a minimum period of seven years from the date of account closure.

**5. RISK FLAGS**
* Compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations is critical to prevent financial crimes and maintain the integrity of the banking system.
* Failure to comply with this policy may result in regulatory penalties, fines, or even legal action.
