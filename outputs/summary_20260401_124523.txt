**SUMMARY**

1. PURPOSE: This policy establishes the requirements for customer due diligence (CDD) procedures to be followed by all retail banking staff when onboarding new customers or reviewing existing customer relationships.

2. KEY POINTS:
* All new customers must provide valid government-issued photo identification and address verification using utility bills or bank statements not older than three months.
* Corporate customers must provide certificate of incorporation, memorandum of association, and board resolution in addition to individual ID documents.
* Customers are classified into three risk categories: low, medium, and high, with high-risk customers requiring enhanced due diligence procedures.
* Enhanced due diligence includes source of funds verification, senior management approval for account opening, more frequent transaction monitoring, and annual relationship reviews.
* All customer due diligence records must be maintained for a minimum period of seven years from the date of account closure.

3. WHO IT AFFECTS: This policy applies to all retail banking operations, including savings accounts, current accounts, fixed deposits, personal loans, and credit card applications, covering both individual and corporate customers.

4. ACTION ITEMS:
* Complete mandatory AML training annually for customer onboarding staff.
* Ensure compliance with this policy within respective branches as a branch manager.

5. RISK FLAGS:
* Failure to maintain accurate and up-to-date customer due diligence records may result in regulatory non-compliance and potential fines or penalties.
* Inadequate risk classification of customers may lead to inadequate enhanced due diligence procedures, increasing the risk of financial crime.
