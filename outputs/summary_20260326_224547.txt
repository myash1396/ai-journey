**1. PURPOSE**
This document outlines the requirements for customer due diligence (CDD) procedures for retail banking staff when onboarding new customers or reviewing existing relationships.

**2. KEY POINTS**

* New customers must provide valid government-issued photo identification and address verification.
* Corporate customers must provide additional documentation, including certificate of incorporation and board resolution.
* Customers are classified into three risk categories: low, medium, and high.
* High-risk customers require enhanced due diligence procedures, including source of funds verification and senior management approval.
* All CDD records must be maintained for at least seven years.

**3. WHO IT AFFECTS**
All retail banking staff involved in customer onboarding, as well as corporate customers and individual customers applying for new accounts or services.

**4. ACTION ITEMS**

* Retail banking staff must complete mandatory AML training annually.
* Branch managers are responsible for ensuring compliance with this policy within their respective branches.

**5. RISK FLAGS**

* Compliance risk: failure to maintain accurate CDD records or implement enhanced due diligence procedures for high-risk customers.
* Regulatory risk: potential fines or penalties if CDD requirements are not met.
* Operational risk: increased risk of fraud or money laundering if inadequate due diligence is performed on new customers.
