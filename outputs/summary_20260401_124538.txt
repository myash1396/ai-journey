• All retail banking staff must follow established requirements for customer due diligence procedures when onboarding new customers or reviewing existing customer relationships.
• This policy applies to all retail banking operations, including savings accounts, current accounts, fixed deposits, personal loans, and credit card applications, covering both individual and corporate customers.
• New customers must provide valid government-issued photo identification, acceptable documents being passport, national identity card, or driving license, with address verification using utility bills or bank statements not older than three months.
• Corporate customers must provide certificate of incorporation, memorandum of association, and board resolution in addition to the above requirements.
• Customers are classified into three risk categories: low risk, medium risk, and high risk, based on factors such as income sources and credit history.
• High-risk customers require enhanced due diligence procedures, including source of funds verification, senior management approval for account opening, and more frequent transaction monitoring and annual relationship reviews.
• All customer due diligence records must be maintained for a minimum period of seven years from the date of account closure and stored securely, with access available to regulatory authorities upon request.
• All staff involved in customer onboarding must complete mandatory AML training annually.
• Branch managers are responsible for ensuring compliance with this policy within their respective branches.
