## 📋 REQUIREMENT SUMMARY
This BRD outlines an automated retail loan application processing system for a bank, intended to replace the existing manual loan review process handled by loan officers. The system covers the end-to-end loan lifecycle including application submission, eligibility checks, document verification, approval workflows, and disbursement. Key business goals include reducing processing time from 5 days to 24 hours, improving customer experience through automated notifications, and achieving a 30% reduction in operational costs. The solution requires integration with CIBIL, core banking, document verification, and notification services.

## 👤 USER STORIES

1. **As a** retail customer, **I want to** submit my loan application online with all required details **so that** I can apply for a loan without visiting a branch.

2. **As a** retail customer, **I want to** receive automated SMS and email updates on my application status **so that** I stay informed throughout the loan process without calling the bank.

3. **As a** retail customer, **I want to** receive a document upload reminder within 48 hours **so that** I do not miss submission deadlines and avoid application delays.

4. **As a** loan processing system, **I want to** automatically validate PAN number format and reject duplicate applications within 30 days **so that** data integrity is maintained and fraudulent or redundant submissions are prevented.

5. **As a** loan processing system, **I want to** automatically check applicant eligibility against age, income, credit score and EMI criteria **so that** only qualified applications proceed to the next stage.

6. **As a** branch manager, **I want to** review and approve loan applications between Rs 5 lakhs and Rs 25 lakhs **so that** mid-range loan decisions are made with appropriate human oversight.

7. **As a** credit committee member, **I want to** review and approve loan applications above Rs 25 lakhs **so that** high-value lending decisions receive the required level of scrutiny and authorization.

8. **As a** loan processing system, **I want to** auto-approve applications under Rs 5 lakhs when all eligibility criteria are met **so that** low-risk loans are processed within the 24-hour target without manual intervention.

9. **As a** disbursement officer, **I want to** disburse approved loan amounts only to the applicant's verified bank account within 48 hours of approval **so that** funds are transferred securely and compliantly.

10. **As a** loan processing system, **I want to** automatically generate a loan agreement upon approval **so that** legal documentation is produced accurately and without manual effort.

## 📐 BUSINESS RULES

BR-001: Loan applications must include all mandatory fields — Full name, Date of birth, PAN number, Monthly income, Loan amount requested, Loan purpose, and Employment type — before submission is accepted.

BR-002: PAN number format must be validated by the system prior to accepting the application submission.

BR-003: Duplicate applications from the same applicant submitted within 30 days must be automatically rejected by the system.

BR-004: Applicant must be a minimum of 21 years of age at the time of application.

BR-005: Applicant must not exceed 65 years of age at the time of loan maturity.

BR-006: Applicant's minimum monthly income must be Rs 25,000 to be eligible.

BR-007: Applicant's CIBIL credit score must be a minimum of 700 to qualify.

BR-008: Applicant's total existing loan EMI obligations must not exceed 50% of their monthly income.

BR-009: Required documents for verification are ID proof, Address proof, Income proof, and Bank statements.

BR-010: If documents are not uploaded within 48 hours of application submission, the system must send an automated reminder to the applicant.

BR-011: All submitted documents must be verified within 24 hours of their submission.

BR-012: Loan applications under Rs 5 lakhs must be auto-approved if all eligibility criteria are satisfied.

BR-013: Loan applications between Rs 5 lakhs and Rs 25 lakhs require branch manager approval.

BR-014: Loan applications above Rs 25 lakhs require credit committee approval.

BR-015: The applicant must be notified of the loan decision within 24 hours of the decision being made.

BR-016: Approved loans must be disbursed within 48 hours of approval.

BR-017: Disbursement must only be made to a verified bank account belonging to the applicant.

BR-018: A loan agreement must be automatically generated by the system upon loan approval.

BR-019: The system must comply with RBI guidelines for digital lending.

BR-020: All data must be encrypted both at rest and in transit.

## ⚠️ EDGE CASES

EC-001: The BRD does not define what happens when a duplicate application is rejected — it is unclear whether the customer receives a notification explaining the rejection reason and the 30-day restriction.

EC-002: The maximum age rule states 65 years at loan maturity, but the BRD does not specify the maximum loan tenure, making it impossible to calculate the age-at-maturity check without this data.

EC-003: There is no defined behavior for partial document submissions — it is unclear whether the application proceeds with available documents or is held entirely until all documents are uploaded.

EC-004: The BRD does not specify what happens if the CIBIL integration is unavailable at the time of eligibility check — whether the application is paused, queued, or manually reviewed is not addressed.

EC-005: There is no defined escalation path if a branch manager does not act on an application within a specific timeframe, creating a potential SLA breach for the 24-hour processing target.

EC-006: The BRD does not address what happens when the credit committee does not reach a decision within the 24-hour notification window, particularly for applications above Rs 25 lakhs which may require extended deliberation.

EC-007: The behavior for applications where the applicant's credit score is exactly 700 or income is exactly Rs 25,000 (boundary values) is not explicitly confirmed as pass or fail.

EC-008: There is no mention of what happens if the loan agreement is rejected or not signed by the customer after approval — whether the approval lapses or is held is undefined.

EC-009: The BRD does not address re-application rules after a rejection — whether a rejected applicant must wait 30 days or can reapply immediately is not specified.

EC-010: There is no handling defined for cases where the applicant's verified bank account belongs to a different bank, which may affect core banking integration and disbursement timelines.

## ❓ DEVELOPER QUESTIONS

DQ-001: What is the exact PAN number format validation rule to be applied — is it the standard 10-character alphanumeric format (AAAAA9999A), and should it also be validated against a government database?

DQ-002: How is a duplicate application identified — by PAN number alone, or by a combination of PAN number, loan amount, and loan purpose?

DQ-003: What is the CIBIL API specification, authentication method, request/response structure, and expected response time SLA for credit score retrieval?

DQ-004: What is the data contract and integration method (REST/SOAP/batch) for the core banking system used for disbursement?

DQ-005: Which document verification service is being used, and does it support automated OCR-based verification or does it require manual review as a fallback?

DQ-006: What is the notification service provider for SMS and email, and are there pre-approved templates that must be used for RBI compliance?

DQ-007: What is the maximum loan tenure offered, and is it fixed or variable by loan type? This is required to implement the age-at-maturity eligibility rule (BR-005).

DQ-008: Should the 48-hour document reminder be a one-time notification or repeated at defined intervals until documents are uploaded or the application is cancelled?

DQ-009: Is there a defined SLA for branch manager and credit committee actions, and should the system auto-escalate if the SLA is breached?

DQ-010: What Pega case type hierarchy is expected — should each loan application be a single top-level case with stages, or should document verification and approval be modelled as child cases?

DQ-011: Should the auto-generated loan agreement be a PDF produced via a Pega correspondence template, or does it need to be generated by an external document management system?

DQ-012: What is the data source for verifying the applicant's bank account, and what constitutes a "verified" bank account — penny drop verification, manual check, or pre-existing core banking record?

## 🚨 RISK FLAGS

RF-001: RBI Digital Lending Guidelines compliance is mentioned but not detailed anywhere in the BRD. Specific requirements such as Key Fact Statement (KFS), cooling-off period, and direct disbursement to borrower account rules must be explicitly mapped to system behavior to avoid regulatory non-compliance.

RF-002: The 24-hour processing target for applications requiring credit committee approval (above Rs 25 lakhs) is operationally unrealistic, as committee reviews typically require scheduling and deliberation. This creates a contractual SLA risk.

RF-003: CIBIL integration introduces a single point of failure for the eligibility check stage. The absence of a fallback or retry mechanism in the BRD means any CIBIL downtime could block all applications from progressing, violating the 99.9% uptime requirement.

RF-004: Disbursement to a verified bank account without explicit penny drop or two-factor verification creates a financial fraud risk, particularly if the account verification process is not clearly defined and auditable.

RF-005: The BRD does not mention data retention, audit trail, or right-to-erasure policies. In the context of RBI guidelines and IT Act compliance, the absence of these requirements is a regulatory risk.

RF-006: Encrypting data at rest and in transit is stated as a requirement but no encryption standards are specified (e.g., AES-256, TLS 1.2+). Without defined standards, implementation may not meet RBI cybersecurity framework requirements.

RF-007: The system is required to handle 1,000 concurrent applications, but no load testing or performance benchmarking criteria are defined. If Pega infrastructure is not sized appropriately, this could result in system degradation under peak load.

RF-008: Auto-approval of loans under Rs 5 lakhs based solely on system-evaluated criteria, without any human review checkpoint, carries credit risk if the eligibility rules do not account for all risk factors considered under the bank's credit policy.
