• The policy establishes requirements for customer due diligence procedures for retail banking staff when onboarding new customers or reviewing existing relationships.
• The policy applies to all retail banking operations, including savings accounts, current accounts, fixed deposits, personal loans, and credit card applications, covering both individual and corporate customers.
• All new customers must provide valid government-issued photo identification (passport, national ID card, or driving license) and address verification using utility bills or bank statements not older than three months.
• Corporate customers must also provide a certificate of incorporation, memorandum of association, and board resolution.
• Customers are classified into three risk categories: low-risk (salaried individuals with regular income sources and established credit history), medium-risk (self-employed individuals and small business owners), and high-risk (politically exposed persons, non-resident customers, and those operating in high-risk industries).
• High-risk customers require enhanced due diligence procedures, including source of funds verification, senior management approval for account opening, more frequent transaction monitoring, and annual relationship reviews.
• All customer due diligence records must be maintained for a minimum period of seven years from the date of account closure, stored securely, and made available to regulatory authorities upon request.
• All staff involved in customer onboarding must complete mandatory AML training annually.
• Branch managers are responsible for ensuring compliance with this policy within their respective branches.
