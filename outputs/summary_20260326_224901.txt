The Customer Due Diligence Policy sets requirements for retail banking staff when onboarding new customers or reviewing existing relationships. All new customers must provide valid government-issued photo identification and address verification, while corporate customers must provide additional documentation such as certificates of incorporation. Customers are classified into three risk categories (low, medium, high) based on factors like income source and credit history. High-risk customers require enhanced due diligence procedures, including source of funds verification and senior management approval. The policy also outlines record-keeping requirements and staff responsibilities, including mandatory AML training and compliance with the policy within respective branches.
