Here is the structured summary of the document:

**1. PURPOSE**
This document outlines the requirements for Customer Due Diligence (CDD) procedures when onboarding new customers or reviewing existing customer relationships in the retail banking division.

**2. KEY POINTS**

* All new customers must provide valid government-issued photo identification and address verification using utility bills or bank statements not older than three months.
* Corporate customers must also provide certificate of incorporation, memorandum of association, and board resolution.
* Customers are classified into three risk categories: Low, Medium, and High risk.
* High-risk customers require enhanced due diligence procedures, including source of funds verification, senior management approval for account opening, and more frequent transaction monitoring.

**3. WHO IT AFFECTS**
All retail banking staff involved in customer onboarding, as well as corporate customers seeking to establish relationships with the bank.

**4. ACTION ITEMS**

* Retail banking staff must complete mandatory AML training annually.
* Branch managers are responsible for ensuring compliance with this policy within their respective branches.

**5. RISK FLAGS**
None noted explicitly, but the emphasis on enhanced due diligence procedures for high-risk customers suggests potential risks related to non-compliance with anti-money laundering and terrorist financing regulations.
