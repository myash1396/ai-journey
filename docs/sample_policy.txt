CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE POLICY - RETAIL BANKING DIVISION

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes the requirements for customer due diligence (CDD) 
procedures to be followed by all retail banking staff when onboarding new 
customers or reviewing existing customer relationships.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all retail banking operations including savings accounts,
current accounts, fixed deposits, personal loans and credit card applications.
It covers both individual and corporate customers.

3. CUSTOMER IDENTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
All new customers must provide valid government issued photo identification.
Acceptable documents include passport, national identity card or driving license.
Address verification must be completed using utility bills or bank statements 
not older than three months. Corporate customers must additionally provide 
certificate of incorporation, memorandum of association and board resolution.

4. RISK CLASSIFICATION
Customers are classified into three risk categories. Low risk customers include
salaried individuals with regular income sources and established credit history.
Medium risk customers include self employed individuals and small business owners.
High risk customers include politically exposed persons, non resident customers
and those operating in high risk industries such as cash intensive businesses.

5. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE
High risk customers require enhanced due diligence procedures. This includes
source of funds verification, senior management approval for account opening,
more frequent transaction monitoring and annual relationship reviews.

6. RECORD KEEPING
All customer due diligence records must be maintained for a minimum period of
seven years from the date of account closure. Records must be stored securely
and made available to regulatory authorities upon request.

7. STAFF RESPONSIBILITIES
All staff involved in customer onboarding must complete mandatory AML training
annually. Branch managers are responsible for ensuring compliance with this
policy within their respective branches.
